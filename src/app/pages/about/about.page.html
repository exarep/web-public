About Exarep

A Texas retail electric provider built on transparency, reliability, and putting customers first.

Also built entirely on Red Hat technologies and a healthy dose of imagination.

Our Mission

Disclaimer: Exarep is a fictitious company created solely to demonstrate enterprise application architecture using Red Hat technologies including OpenShift, Quarkus, Kafka, and more. We do not sell electricity, we do not have a PUCT certificate, and our "customer service team" is a Quarkus REST endpoint that returns JSON.

Exarep was founded with a simple belief: Texans deserve an electricity provider that is honest about pricing, responsive when they need help, and committed to delivering reliable power.

As a hypothetical Retail Electric Provider in the deregulated Texas market, we would purchase wholesale electricity and sell it directly to homes and businesses across the state. We would work with your local TDSP to ensure seamless delivery. If, you know, we existed.

What does exist is the full technology stack behind this application — built on Red Hat OpenShift, Quarkus, Angular, Kafka, and PostgreSQL — demonstrating how a real REP could be built with enterprise open-source software.

How the Texas Electricity Market Actually Works

This part is real. We did our homework even if the company is made up.

Deregulation

Texas deregulated its electricity market in 2002, giving consumers the power to choose their Retail Electric Provider. The generation, transmission, distribution, and retail sale of electricity are handled by separate entities.

Your TDSP

Your Transmission and Distribution Service Provider (also called a TDU) owns the poles, wires, and meters that deliver electricity to your home. Regardless of which REP you choose, your TDSP remains the same and delivery charges are regulated.

ERCOT

The Electric Reliability Council of Texas manages the flow of electricity across the Texas grid and facilitates the retail market, including customer switching between REPs.

Seamless Switching

When you switch to a real REP, they submit an enrollment request through ERCOT. Your TDSP handles the transition and your power stays on. When you "switch" to Exarep, a JSON object gets persisted to a PostgreSQL database. Your power is unaffected because we were never involved.
